Walt Disney

The producer of the first synchronized sound cartoon

Below is the timeline of Walt Disney's life:

  • 1901 - Walter Elias Disney is born in Chicago
  • 1906 - The disney family moves to Marceline, MO, where Walt has an idyllic childhood on a farm and develops a strong interest in drawing
  • 1910 - Because of his father's poor health, the family sels the farm
  • 1911 - The family moves to Kansas City, MO, where Walt begins to deliver newspappers
  • 1917 - Walt attends evening classes at the Chicago Academy of Fine Arts hoping to become a newspapper cartonist
  • 1918 - Walt lies about his age to be accepted into the American Ambulance Corps and serves in France following the end of World War I.
  • 1919 - Walt returns to the US, move to Kansas City and gets a job at the Pesmen-Rubin Commercial Art Studio for 50$ a month
  • 1920 - Walt meets Ub Iwerks and forms Iwerks-Disney Commercial Artists.The company fails after one month.
  • 1922 - Walt incorporates Laugh-O-gram Films,Inc. with $15,000 from backers.
  • 1923 - Laugh-O-gram goes bankrupt. Walt moves to Hollywood to become a director. Together with his brother Roy,they established the Disney Brothers Studio
  • 1926 - Walt and Roy rename the company Walt Disney Studio and move it to new location on Hyperion Avenue
  • 1928 - Mickey Mouse is created, an innovative cartoon that synchronizes sound and animation
  • 1937 - Disney Studios develops an sophisticated multiplane camera that simultaneously shoots several levels of cels and backgrounds and gives depth to its films
  • 1939 - Walt wins an honorary Academy Award for Snow White and Seven Dwarfs
  • 1940 - The release of Pinocchio and Fantastia
  • 1941 - Disney Studios releases Dumbo
  • 1942 - Disney Studios releases Bambi , the most naturalistic animation film to date
  • 1944 - Disney re-releases Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs succesfuly
  • 1948 - Disney Studios premieres Seal Island, the first of the "True-Life-Adventures" series
  • 1955 - Lady and the Tramp is the first animated feature filmed in wide-screen CinemaScope technology
  • 1964 - Mary Poppins is released and is later nominated for 13 Academy Awards
  • 1966 - Walt dies on december 15

"If you can dream it, you can do it." --Walt Disney
